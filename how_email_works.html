So I just add my subject line, my recepients, and my body, and viola- Send!

But, how does email actually work- surley it's not "magic" or some "geeky wizardy".

Well, it's actually quite simple!

The user interfaces with an email client, such as gmail or yahoo, through a website. Users each have their own unique address. When the user composes their email, there are a few things of note:

To : used for main recipients of an email

CC (Carbon Copy) : used for secondary recipeients; they can still see the other recpients

BCC (Blind Carbon Copy): used by the sender to "blind" the recpeients from seeing each other

Other than that, the user simply hits sends, and this is where the fun begins!

1. The email client will communicate with YOUR own server using SMTP, which is the protocol for outgoing mail.
2. Your email server communicates with the intended target's server, through DNS (which finds IP) and perhaps several SMTP server through "hopping"
3. The email is then stored in a mailbox
4. The recipient does not get the mail directly to their device, unless they request to view it [like clicking on the mail in the app]
